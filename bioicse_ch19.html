Waste Generation And Management

WHAT IS A WASTE?

Waste is any substance that is discarded after primary use, or it is worthless,defective and of no use.

CATEGORIES OF WASTES

There are broadly five categories of wastes. They are -
1. Domestic Waste given out from homes,
2. Industrial Waste given out from factories,thermal plants etc.,
3. Agricultural Waste that is left behind after obtaining the consumable parts,
4. Municipal Waste which includes the overall waste given out from the township,and
5. E-Waste(electronic waste) from electronic and electrical equipment.

1. DOMESTIC WASTE :

Domestic wastes include:
i. Kitchen Waste: Several items such as:
- peelings of vegetables and fruits,
- Washing of pulses,rice etc. before cooking,
- Any leftovers in the food dishes
ii. Plastics: plastic packings and wrappings, discarded toys etc..
iii. Glass: broken mirrors, glass utensils, electric bulbs etc..
iv. Rags: a huge variety of old and torn clothes,towels, bedsheets, blankets, cushions etc..
v. Paper: Daily newspapersand magazines

2. INDUSTRIAL WASTE:

Industries use raw materials and give out a lot of wastes. some of these are as follows:
i. Mining Operations: A lot of wastes are produced while extracting copper, silver, gold, zinc, iron, coal etc..Such waste are mainly called mine tailing.The mine tailing mixed with other materials can be used for making tiles, masonry cement etc..
ii. Cement Industries: The waste given out may be solid, liquid or gaseous.
- The solid wastes are either used in construction activities or dumped in land-fills.
- The gaseous waste include flyash which can be used in making variety of building materials like bricks, concrete, roofing sheets etc..
iii. Oil Refineries: While refining crude oil, a lot of poisonous gases and liquids are produced.
- Gaseous waste is cleaned by passing through cleaners and purified part is released into the atmosphere whereas liquid wastes are processed and suitably dumped.
iv. Construction Units:These produce huge quantities of waste stone, pebbles, broken bricks and wooden wastes. These are mostly dumped in land-fills.

3. AGRICULTURAL WASTE:

The agricultural waste mainly includes:
-Agricultural residue: The plant parts left after obtaining the usable portions.Much of these is used as animal feed.
-Bagasse is the plant residue after extracting the sugarcane juice.
-Pesticides and Fertilizers
-Animal Wastes include cow dung and other faecal matter which is used in making manure.

4. MUNICIPAL WASTE:

The municipal waste includes the following:
- Household discharge of excreta and kitchen washings.
- Discharge from public toilets, hospitals, hotels, restaurants etc..

- All this waste is carried through sewers and is called sewage.This sewage is separated into:
Degradable parts and
Non-degradable parts.
- Septic tanks are used to degrade the degradable part. Non-degradable part is dumped or buried at safe places.

5. E-WASTE:

e-waste is the abbreviation of electronic waste.
- includes old televisions, refrigerator, mobile phones, batteries, electronic toys etc..
e-waste may contain harmful substances as well as valuable substances:
- Harmful substances include lead, cadmium, mercury etc..
- Valuable substances include silver, gold, copper etc..

6. Science teaching and research laboratories waste:

- includes broken or discarded glass apparatus, condemned machines, waste chemicals, animal and plant wastes.

METHODS OF SAFE DISPOSAL OF WASTES

1. SEGREGATION

- means separating the refuse into three categories- reusable, degradable and non-degradable parts.
- Reusable wastes include items such as papers(newspapers, old books, discarded notebooks). The metallic components can also be separated and reused.
- Degradable such as organic wastes (vegetables and fruit peelings) can be decomposed into manure using micro-organisms. This process is called biodegradation.
- Non-degradable parts such as plastics can be dumped.

2. COMPOSTING

- is the process of putting the waste organic matter to decay so that it can be used for fertilizing the agricultural land.
- the fertilizing material produced from composting organic waste is called compost.
- one of the latest methods is to use dry composting toilets, which are hygenic and cost-effective way of human waste disposal.

3. DRAINAGE

- A proper system of drains is required to carry away all kinds of fluid wastes other than sewage.

TREATMENT OF EFFLUENTS BEFORE DISCHARGE

- The Industrial and municipal waste waters are treated in Effluent treatment plants before disposing them off in water bodies. This is done in three parts:
1. Primary treatment consists of separation of large debris by sedimentation in large tanks.
2. Secondary treatment consist of oxidising the organic matter using micro-organisms producing co2 gas and a solid precipitated material called sludge which can be used as manure.
3. Tertiary treatment involves removal of dissolved chemicals, metals and pathogens and then the waste water is discharged into the natural water or used in irrigation.

4. INCINERATION

is the disposal of waste by burning.
Reduces the volume and weight of the waste and renders toxic waste into less toxic waste.
Releases fumes and harmful substances.
Should be carried out at very high temparatures, using pollution control devices and away from residential areas.
The ash leftover requires much less land-fill space.
Electricity can be generated through heat produced during burning.

5.SCRUBBERS

are devices to remove gaseous and particulate air pollutants.

6. ELECTROSTATIC PRECIPITATORS

The dirty air is passed through a chamber containing electrically charged plates.
As the naturally charged air particles pass through the charged plates of the precipitator they get collected on the oppositely charged plates and the clean gas passes out.
Used in thermal plants.
